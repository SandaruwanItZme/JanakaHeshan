About

Two crafts, one obsession: things that work.

I started out with a screwdriver, not a keyboard. Long before I wrote my first line of code, I was pulling apart old desktops in Kegalle to figure out why they wouldn't boot — reseating RAM, replacing thermal paste, chasing dead capacitors across motherboards that everyone else had given up on.

That instinct — trace the fault, isolate the variable, fix the real problem — turned out to be exactly the mindset web development rewards. Today I split my time between hardware diagnostics and building fast, resilient websites for small businesses and freelancers who need someone who understands both the server and the box it's plugged into.

I care about systems that hold up under real use: a PC that survives a monsoon power cut, a website that loads in under a second on a shared 4G connection. That's the standard I build to.
